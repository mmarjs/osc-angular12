1. Why this policy exists?

Ocean Service Center (the Company) understands the importance of protecting personal information and is committed to complying with the Data Protection Laws, meaning all the laws applicable to the Company that regulate how organizations must collect and handle Personal Data (any information relating to an identified o identifiable individual, it can be anything from a name, photo, email address, bank details, IP address, or a combination of the data that could directly or indirectly identify the person).

Ocean Service Center needs to collect and Process (meaning anything that is done with the Personal Data, including store, transfer, or delete it) Personal Data about the people it interacts with, to carry out its business. These can include users, clients, third-party suppliers, business contacts, employees and other people the organization has a relationship with or may need to contact (Data Subjects).

This Data Protection Policy (the Policy) describes how Personal Data must be collected and Processed to meet the Company’s data protection standards. This Policy ensures Ocean Service Center complies with the Data Protection Laws, protects Data Subject’s rights, and protects itself from the risks of a data breach. Ocean Service Center is committed to ensuring that its employees are appropriately trained and supported to achieve compliance with the Data Protection Laws.


2. The Data Protection Laws’ Principles

The Data Protection Laws are generally underpinned by seven important principles: Lawfulness, fairness and transparency; Purpose limitation; Data minimization; Accuracy; Storage limitation; Integrity and Confidentiality; and Accountability. According to these principles, Personal Data must be:


1. Processed fairly and lawfully

2. Collected and Processed with transparency

3. Obtained only for specific, lawfulpurposes

4. Adequate, relevant and notexcessive

5. Accurate and kept up todate

6. Held for no longer thannecessary

7. Processed in accordance with the rights of DataSubjects

8. Protected in appropriateways

9. Destroyed when lawfully requested by the affected individual

10. Transferred only if an adequate level ofprotection is ensured

11. Data controllers and Processors must be able to demonstrate compliance with the Data Protection Laws.


3. Policy Scope and Risks

This Policy applies to the offices of Ocean Service Center, all its employees, whether permanent or temporal, and all its contractors, consultants, suppliers and any other people working on behalf of Ocean Service Center.

It applies to all Personal Data that the Company collects and Processes, including names of individuals, date of birth, username, photos, social media accounts, postal or e mail addresses, telephone numbers, Driver’s License, Taxpayer identification number, or any other official identification number or document, IP address, geographic location, credit card information, bank account information, and any other information or document relating to identifiable individuals.

This policy helps to protect Ocean Service Center from data security risks, including data breaches (for instance, information being given out inappropriately), failing to protect Data Subject’s rights, and reputational damage (the Company could suffer if an unauthorized person successfully gained access to Personal Data stored by the Company).

5. Responsibilities

Everyone who works for or with Ocean Service Center has some responsibility for ensuring that Personal Data is collected and Processed appropriately. Each employee who handles Personal Data must ensure that it is Processed in line with this Policy and the Data Protection Laws and its principles.

However, these people have key areas of responsibility:

  • The CEO, Robert Smith, is ultimately responsible for ensuring that Ocean Service Center meets its legal obligations.

  • The Data Protection Supervisor, Robert Smith is responsible for:

  • Keeping the CEO updated about the Data Protection Laws, risks and issues.

  • Reviewing all data protection procedures and related policies, making sure the Privacy Policy and any related notices are reviewed periodically and updated when necessary.

  • Arranging data protection training for the employees and handling their questions regarding this Policy.

  • Dealing with Data Subjects’ Requests (requests from individuals regarding the Personal Data that Ocean Service Center holds about them) in accordance with this Policy.

  • Reviewing any contracts or agreements with third parties that may Process the Personal Data collected by or for the Company.

  • Maintaining the Breach Response Policy and taking care of any breach.

  • The IT, Robert Smith is responsible for:

  • Ensuring all systems, services and equipment used for collecting and Processing data meet acceptable security standards.

  • Performing regular checks and scans to ensure that the security software is functioning properly.

  • Evaluating any third-party services that the Company is considering to collect or Process data (for instance, cloud computing services).

  • Establishing IT Guidelines (to ensure Personal Data protection while using the electronic devices and systems, including a password policy) for the employees, and oversee employees’ compliance.

  • Training the employees to follow the IT Guidelines, and to identify and avoid security breaches, email and other internet scams.

  • The Marketing Manager, Robert Smith is responsible for:

  • Approving any data protection statements included in the Company’s communications such as emails and newsletters.

  • Addressing any marketing mailing objections and updating the marketing mailing list, to make sure Personal Data from people who opted-out is deleted permanently.

  • Updating the mailing list to incorporate users and other business contact who have consented, and keeping records of consent.

  • Ensuring that the Company’s marketing initiatives and communications abide by the Data Protection Laws.


6. Data Protection Officer

The appointment of a Data Protection Officer is obligatory if: (1) processing is carried out by a public authority; or (2) the “core activities” of a data controller or data processor either require “the regular and systematic monitoring of Data Subjects on a large scale,” or consist of processing of Special Categories of Data (race and ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, political opinions, trade union memberships, biometric or genetic data, health data, and data related to sexual life or orientation) or data about criminal convictions “on a large scale.”

Ocean Service Center is a private Company, which core activities does not include the regular and systematic monitoring of Data Subjects on a large scale, nor collects or Processes Special Categories of Data, or data about criminal convictions on a large scale. Therefore, Ocean Service Center does not need to appoint a Data Protection Officer.


7. Data Protection Guidelines

Ocean Service Center will provide this Data Protection Policy to all its employees to help them understand their responsibilities when collecting or Processing Personal Data. The Company will conduct training sessions or will provide informative materials to inform employees of their responsibilities.

All employees are responsible for ensuring that they comply with this Policy and with the requirements of the Data Protection Laws. Employees should request help from the Data Protection Supervisor or the IT, if they are unsure about any aspect of data protection.

The Data Protection Laws’ requirements will be considered at the initial design stages of any new project or process to be implemented that involves Personal Data, as well as throughout the lifecycle of the relevant Personal Data Processing.


7.1 Storage

These rules describe how Personal Data should be safely stored. Questions about storing data safely can be directed to the IT and the Data Protection Supervisor.

These guidelines apply to Personal Data that is usually stored electronically but has been printed out for some reason:

  • When Personal Data is stored on paper, it should be kept in a secure place where unauthorized people cannot see it. When not being used, the paper or files should be kept in a locked drawer or filing cabinet.

  • All physical documents containing Personal Data must be physically destroyed using a paper shredder, once they are not needed.

  • At the end of the day, printers must be checked, and all documents (that contain Personal Data) left there, should be stored or destroyed. Similarly, all files must be properly stored, and file cabinets locked.

When Personal Data is stored electronically, it must be protected from unauthorized access, accidental deletion and malicious hacking attempts:

  • Personal Data should be protected by strong passwords that are changed regularly, and never shared between employees, unless necessary.

  • If Personal Data is stored on removable media (like a CD or a flash drive), these should be kept locked away securely when not being used.

  • Personal Data should only be stored on designated drives and servers, and should only be uploaded to approved cloud computing services. Personal Data should never be saved directly to personal laptops or other mobile devices, like tablets or smart phones.

  • Servers containing personal data should be sited in a secure location, away from general office space. Personal Data should be backed up frequently and backups should be tested regularly.

  • All servers and computers containing data should be protected by approved security software and a firewall.


7.2 Use

It is when Personal Data is accessed and used that is at the greatest risk of loss, corruption or theft. The following guidelines will be used when Personal Data is being Processed:

  • When working with Personal Data, employees should ensure the screens of their computers are always locked when left unattended (the computer screen can be easily locked by putting it to “sleep”).

  • The only people able to access Personal Data should be those who need it for their work. Personal Data should not be shared informally, and should not be disclosed by any means to unauthorized people, either within the Company or externally.

  • Only share Personal Data with authorized third parties when it is absolutely necessary. If you are not sure, it is your obligation to ask to a supervisor before sharing Personal Data.

  • Always access and update the central copy of any Personal Data.


7.3 Accuracy

The Data Protection Laws requires Ocean Service Center to take reasonable steps to ensure Personal Data is kept accurate and up to date. It is the responsibility of all employees who work with Personal Data to take reasonable steps to ensure it is kept as accurate and up to date as possible.

  • Ocean Service Center will make it easy for Data Subjects to update their Personal Data in Ocean Service Center’s system.

  • Personal Data should be regularly reviewed and updated if it is found to be out of date. If no longer required, it should be deleted and disposed of properly.

  • Marketing databases should be checked regularly.


8. Data Subjects’ Rights

All individuals who are the subject of Personal Data held by Ocean Service Center are entitled to:

  • Have access to Ocean Service Center Privacy Policy (which is available at the website), to be informed how their Personal data is being collected and processed, and how to exercise their rights.

  • Make a Data Subject Request to:

    • Ask Ocean Service Center whether it holds any Personal Data relating to them and, if so, to receive a description and a copy of that Personal Data.

    • Request the rectification of inaccurate Personal Data. Users are responsible for maintaining the accuracy of the information they submit to Ocean Service Center, they may access and correct of their personal information through their online account. This right of rectification is not absolute and certain exceptions may apply.

    • Request the deletion of their Personal Data. This right is not absolute, and it does not apply if Processing is necessary to exercise a legal right, to comply with a legal obligation, or to establish, exercise or defend a legal claim, among other exceptions.

    • Make a request to restrict the Processing of their Personal Data. This right is not absolute and certain exceptions apply.

    • Request their Personal Data to be transferred to a third party. This right only applies to information that the individual has provided to the Company.

    • Object to the Processing of their Personal Data. Individuals have an absolute right to stop their Personal Data from being used for direct marketing. The Marketing Manager will update the marketing mailing list to remove those individuals who opted-out from marketing communications.


9. Data Subjects’ Requests

Data Subject Requests from individuals should be made by email or by regular mail, addressed to the Data Protection Supervisor, who will handle the requests. The Data Protection Supervisor can supply a standard request form, although individuals do not have to use it.

The Data Protection Supervisor will always verify the identity of anyone making a Data Subject Request before handing over any information or taking any action.

Ocean Service Center will make reasonable efforts to comply with the Data Subject Requests. The Data Protection Supervisor will respond to the Data Subject Request (including copies of the Personal Data, if requested) within 20 days from the request.


10. Disclosing data for other reasons

In certain circumstances, the Data Protection Laws allow Personal Data to be disclosed to law enforcement agencies without the consent of the Data Subject. Under these circumstances, Ocean Service Center will disclose the requested Personal Data. However, the Data Protection Supervisor will ensure that the request is legitimate, seeking assistance from the CEO and from the Company’s legal advisers.


11. Compliance and Review

Breaches of this policy will be investigated, and appropriate actions will be taken. This Policy will be reviewed annually or as business reasons dictate.
